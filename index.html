HOW TO SELL A HAUNTED HOUSE

by: Grady Hendrix

Lousie - a single mother living in San Francisco who spends her entire life runs away from her family - receives a call from her brother Mark - Mom and Dad are dead.

She agrees to come back home and sell their home. The moment she walks in, she realizes something seems off, especially the room filled with her mom's dolls. Louise is welcomed by the sight of Pupkin, her mother's favorite doll, sitting in a chair in front of the TV, watching Home Shopping Network.

A song Louise hadn't thought of in years starts playing in her head:

"Pupkin here! Pupkin here! Everybody laugh! Everybody cheer!"

"Pupkin was a red-and-yellow glove puppet with two stumpy fabric legs dangling down fron his front and two little nubbin arms. His chalk-white plastic face had a big smiling mouth and a little pug nose, and he looked out of the corners of his wide eyes like he was up to some kind of mischief. His mouth and eyes were outlined in thick black lines and he wore a bloodred bodysuit with a pointed hood and a yellow stomach."
